    The 2000 season marked Aikman’s final season as a professional football player.

    Throughout the season, he endured multiple concussions, resulting in a quarterback rotation between Aikman and former Eagles quarterback Randall Cunningham.

    Aikman’s career came to an end in a game against the Washington Redskins, where he sustained his tenth and final concussion after a hit from linebacker LaVar Arrington.

    In the 2001 offseason, Aikman was waived a day before he was set to receive a $70 million/7-year contract extension.

    Consequently, he announced his retirement on April 9. Despite his efforts to find another team, he concluded his career as the Cowboys’ all-time leading passer.

    Following his retirement in 2000, Aikman transitioned to a career as a color commentator for NFL on Fox, a role he held from 2001 to 2021.

    In 2022, he was appointed as the color commentator for ESPN’s Monday Night Football broadcasts.

    Additionally, Aikman co-owned the NASCAR Sprint Cup Series team Hall of Fame Racing from 2005 to 2009.
